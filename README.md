Job Title: Create a website for "Liberation War Archive" using HTML, CSS & JavaScript.
Conditions:
1. Work must be carried out in a safe manner and according to coding standards
2. Open-source resources can be used
3. Written code must be clean & errors free
Equipment:
1. Personal computer/laptop
Software: Web browser and Text editor
Job Objective:
The objective of this job is to design and develop a comprehensive online archive dedicated to
preserving the history and memories of the Liberation War of Bangladesh. The archive will
contain documents, interviews, photographs, and provide a means for users to contribute their
materials or contact the administrators.
Job Scope:
1. Homepage:
a. Introduction to the archive and its significance in Bangladesh's history.
b. Featured sections highlighting important documents, interviews, and photographs.
2. Documents Section:
a. Catalog of historical documents related to the Liberation War, including treaties, declarations,
and official correspondence.
b. Categorization of documents by type, date, and relevance.
c. Detailed view for each document with description, publication date, and source information.
3. Interviews Section:
a. Collection of recorded interviews with individuals who played significant
roles in or were affected by the Liberation War.
b. Structured presentation with biographical details of interviewees and summary of their
testimonies.
c. Ability to listen to or view interviews directly on the website.
4. Photographs Gallery:
a. Gallery showcasing a curated selection of photographs capturing key moments, places, and
people from the Liberation War.
b. Organized by categories such as events, locations, and themes.
c. High-resolution images with zoom functionality and option to download.

5. Contact Form:
a. Form for users to submit inquiries, provide feedback, or contribute materials to the archive.
b. Fields for name, email, subject, message, and file upload for submitting additional documents
or photographs.
c. Contact information for direct communication with the archive administrators.
